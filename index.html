A stylish two bedroom apartment. Ideally situated in the heart of Falmouth, just two minutes walk from; the Maritime Museum, the Discovery Quay events square, where events such as Falmouth Oyster Festival and Falmouth Beer Festival take place annually, and local bars and restaurants. The apartment is ideal for families and couples and is pet friendly. There is also; disabled lift access to the property, a washing machine, kitchen with an oven and fridge, two bedrooms with 1 kingsize bed and the other having 1 double bed, a bathroom with a bath and shower and a modern open plan lounge with a pull-out sofa bed. The apartment is located on the world’s largest natural harbour with an ever-changing vista of yachts and boats of all sizes which provide endless pleasure and can be seen from the cafes and bars located below the apartment. Access is via secure communal doors and a private elevator. A charming inner garden provides a great deal of peace and quiet within this luxury development. The apartment has underfloor heating fitted throughout.

Falmouth Oyster festival

The oyster festival celebrates a unique Cornish fishing tradition, It takes place from the 12th to the 15th of october. and is one of Englands best-loved specialist food festivals.The main attraction of the festival is the cookery demonstrations provided by leading chefs, supported by their respective hotels and restaurants from across Cornwall, who enthusiastically share their culinary skills and cooking tips. BBC Radio Cornwall’s presenters provide the commentary for the cookery demonstrations, the oyster shucking competition and the hot chef challenge, contributing skilful humour and flair on the stage. Almost as unique as the fishing tradition it celebrates, the festival embraces wonderful community spirit and receives generous backing from a variety of Cornwall’s businesses and individuals.

falmouthoysterfestival.co.uk

Falmouth Beer and Real ale festival

Falmouth’s Princess Pavilion plays host to one of the largest and best beer festivals in the South West, organised by CAMRA Kernow (Cornwall’s branch of the Campaign for Real Ale). Falmouth Beer Festival is a celebration of real ale, cider and perry. The full range will be represented at Falmouth from light, citrusy pale ales to pitch black porters! In addition to the huge Cornish Bar, other bars will provide an excellent selection of the finest products of the brewer’s art from all over England, Wales and Scotland. Live music, games and other entertainment will feature throughout the festival. The evenings tend to be lively and busy; the daytime period starts quieter while the numbers and buzz steadily build towards evening! Admission is £10 which includes entry beer tokens your glass and programme.

falmouthbeerfestvial.co.uk

Falmouth Week

Falmouth Week is a major attraction for locals and visitors alike. With its origins in a local sailing regatta dating back at least far as 1837, the Week has evolved into not only a major sailing competition in the SW but also an opportunity for sailors and landlubbers to enjoy the many daytime and evening attractions in the town. Six days of match racing for keelboats, traditional craft and dinghies in the waters off Falmouth Bay, the Carrick Roads and the harbour. Wednesday is Champagne Day, a race for the big yachts with a 25 mile coastal course finishing in the inner harbour, whilst smaller boats race in the Carrick Roads.

falmouthweek.co.uk

Eden project

Only an hours drive from falmouth lies the Eden project, Dubbed the Eighth Wonder of the World by some, the Eden Project is a dramatic global garden housed in tropical biomes that nestle in a crater the size of 30 football pitches. the worlds largest rainforest in captivity. Inside these biomes are stunning plants, exhibitions and contemporary gardens.With a worldwide reputation, and recognised by the British Travel Awards as the Best UK Leisure Attraction 5 years running.
